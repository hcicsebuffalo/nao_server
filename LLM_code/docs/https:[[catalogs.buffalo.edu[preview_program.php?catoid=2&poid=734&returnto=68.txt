Social Work Practice in Serious Illness Care Micro-Credential
This micro-credential, available for advanced year MSW students, is focused on the development of knowledge and skills in care for and communication with people who have serious illnesses and their families. Through a combination of academic coursework, specialized internships and experiential opportunities, you will be well prepared for work with people who have serious physical and mental illnesses, and/or disabilities including intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDDs).
For more information and to apply visit the Office of Micro-Credentials.
Course Requirements
Social Work Practice in Serious Illness Care Core (6 credits)
SW 568LEC - Serious Illness Care Credits: 3
SW 710LEC - Loss and Grief Across the Life Course Credits: 3
Additional Requirements
An internship. Partner organizations, where students have completed their micro-credential internship, have included:
Alzheimer’s Association of WNY
BestSelf Behavioral Health
Canopy of Neighbors
Chautauqua Hospice & Palliative Care
Crossroads House (a comfort care home)
Erie County Medical Center (Supportive and Palliative Medicine Department, Dialysis, Internal Medicine Clinic)
Grassroots Gardens WNY
Hospice Buffalo
Mercy Doula Program at People Inc.
Pathways Program of Niagara Hospice
Roswell Park Comprehensive Cancer Center
Attend all eight classroom-to-field seminar sessions.
Submit reflective self-evaluation.
Total Credit Hours: 6
3.0 GPA required
  Program Note
SW 568 and SW 710 courses are approved for NYSED social work contact hours to renew your license registration. If you have a license in another state, check with your state regulatory board to determine if New York hours will be accepted in your state.
Instruction Method
In Person (no courses offered online)
Average Time to Completion
Two Semesters

Intercultural and Global Competency Micro-Credential
Participating in a global programs experience is a personal and professional development in the students’ college journey. This micro-credential provides an opportunity for you to assess and apply the competencies gained in a global programs experience, virtually or in person and outside the United States. Through hands-on projects, reflection, and cultural immersive/virtual experiences you will be prepared to better articulate your global competencies and the benefits gained through your global experiences.
For more information and to apply visit the Office of Micro-Credentials.
Academic Requirements
Requirements vary by program.
To be considered, you must submit a completed application, updated résumé, personal statement and unofficial transcripts
Interviews are conducted to identify students who are the right fit and appreciative of different cultures and perspectives, and engaged and open to different ways of learning and solving problems. This is an experiential program where you must be able to step outside your comfort zone and engage with peers from a different country and culture.
Overall GPA of 2.5 is required at the time of application. If your GPA currently falls below a 2.5, address this in your personal statement by indicating your plan to increase it prior to participating in the program.
Course Requirements
Intercultural and Global Competency Core (3 credits)
Completion of 3-credit-hour global experience course. Course number will vary depending on your level of study and geographic region of interest.
Each course will include at least one country-specific team project, journaling of experiences and a reflection paper.
Total Credit Hours: 3
3.0 GPA required
  Instruction Method
Hybrid (some of the micro-credential requirements can be fulfilled online)
Average Time to Completion
One Semester

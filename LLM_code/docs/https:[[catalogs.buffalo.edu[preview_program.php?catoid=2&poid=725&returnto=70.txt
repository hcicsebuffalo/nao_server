Communication Campaigns Micro-Credential
The Communication Campaigns micro-credential prepares learners to develop effective messages and campaigns for diverse career fields, including, but not limited to, marketing, health or politics. This program is open to communication graduate students, graduate students in other disciplines and also non-degree seeking individuals, including professionals who wish to improve their skills and marketability. You will learn the principles of persuasive communication and how to apply these principles in specific circumstances and across media platforms, such as social media or print messages. These skills are valued in the workplace and are especially beneficial for individuals in careers that involve communication, sales or dissemination of knowledge.
For more information and to apply visit the Office of Micro-Credentials.
Course Requirements
Communication Campaigns Core (3 credits)
Students must take at least one foundational course:
COM 540SEM - Persuasion and Social Influence Credits: 3
or
COM 629SEM - Top: Credits: 3
Course title:  Entertainment-Education for Health Promotion and Social Change
or
COM 682SEM - Special Topics Credits: 3
Course title:  Audience Analysis and Program Evaluation
Communication Electives (6 credits)
Students must take 2 additional courses, which may be a combination of graduate Communication (COM) electives or additional foundational courses, listed above.
Total Credit Hours: 9
3.0 GPA required
  Instruction Method
In Person (no courses offered online)

Three Semesters

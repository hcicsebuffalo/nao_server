Electrical Engineering PhD
The Electrical Engineering PhD program provides advanced study and training, focusing on cultivating research-level scholars. Graduates of this program showcase their aptitude by generating original and noteworthy contributions to signals, communications and networking, optics and photonics, solid state electronics, or energy systems through their dissertations.

Curriculum
Required Core & Electives (42-60 credits)
Students will work directly with their academic advisor to select courses that are meaningful for their research interests. Students need to complete at least 42 credits of coursework. Up to 36 MS credits can be accepted towards the required 42 credits. This is at the discretion of the advisor and the Graduate School. Students may select courses outside of EE that correlate with their background and research.
